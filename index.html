Mohamed Shawky Sabae

I am a PhD student in the Computer Graphics research group at Eberhard Karls University of Tübingen, supervised by Prof. Dr.-Ing. Hendrik PA Lensch. I am also a scholar at the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems (IMPRS-IS).

My research focuses on inverse rendering and 3D/4D scene reconstruction. I am particularly interested in material-illumination decomposition of dynamic scenes.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Computer Vision Engineer at Rembrand, where I developed generative AI and 3D reconstruction solutions for virtual product placement. I received my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Computer Engineering from Cairo University. My Master's thesis studied neural implicit 3D reconstruction without known camera parameters.

Research Interests: Computer Vision, Computer Graphics, Inverse Rendering, 3D/4D Reconstruction.
